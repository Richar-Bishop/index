Artist Bio

Richard Bishop doesn't fit neatly into a category, and he's not trying to. He's a combat veteran, a single father, and an independent hard rock artist from Fairfield, Iowa, and every one of those things shows up in his music whether he intends it to or not.

His sound is built in Drop-C tuning, rooted in B minor, driven by a deep baritone chest voice that doesn't reach for high notes because it doesn't need to. Post-grunge grit, blues-rock backbone, heavy distorted guitar, dynamic verses that open into hard-hitting choruses. It's the kind of music that feels lived in because it is.

Richard writes every lyric himself. Every creative decision is his. He uses Suno Pro with a custom-trained voice model for production, and he's transparent about that, because the tools don't change what the work costs. Some of these songs took a long time to be ready to write. That's not a selling point. That's just the truth.

His debut album Still Standing dropped May 16, 2026 on all major streaming platforms through Ditto Pro. Eleven tracks. No label. No manager. No budget. Just a story that needed to be told and the stubbornness to see it through. The album was written for the people who've actually lived something, veterans still carrying what they brought home, working people who keep getting the short end, single parents holding it together on no sleep and no help.

It's not a record about being fine. It's a record about still being here anyway.

Richard owns 100% of his masters and copyrights, is registered with ASCAP and SoundExchange, and distributes through Ditto Pro. He is building this career on his own terms, no shortcuts, no apologies, no one else's timeline.

No bullshit. No regrets. No apologies.
